Background and Problem

The general domain of this project is to serve as a reader's resource, intersecting artificial intelligence, digital libraries, and Christian literature. CCEL.org, which hosts a variety of classic Christian texts—many of which are in the public domain—offers valuable resources for readers. The goal of this project is to create an AI-based tool that enhances the reading experience for multiple purposes.
While AI tools for writing and answering questions, such as chatbots and content generators, have become increasingly popular, there remains a noticeable gap in tools specifically designed to enhance the reading process. Current AI applications primarily assist users in generating content or retrieving quick answers, but few offer meaningful support for reading, understanding, and engaging with complex or archaic texts. This is particularly important for Christian literature, where the language and concepts can pose significant challenges for readers.
To address this gap, our project seeks to develop an AI tool that pulls relevant passages and generates helpful insights or explanations in real-time. This tool would be especially beneficial for texts with rich historical context, where readers might struggle with outdated language and complex theological concepts. The goal is to enable users to not only read but also to understand and engage deeply with Christian literature, making the reading experience more accessible and inviting.

Without a tool like this, people may read even less, especially when confronted with difficult or outdated language. Our project aims to make reading more accessible and to promote active learning by offering an AI tool that supports comprehension instead of merely providing answers.
Some other people are tackling this same problem right now. One of the main websites is called mindgrasp.ai. They allow you to upload notes, books, or videos and ask targeted questions about them or simplify the reading or lecture. Storywiz is a site meant for summarizing articles and making them easier and faster to read. ReadPartner is an AI assistant that creates summaries for various content types. It offers custom digests in multiple languages, detailed analytics, and delivery options, simplifying media consumption for individuals and professionals. There are even some for research papers specifically like ResearchGTP, an open-source LLM-based research assistant tool that enables users to converse with research papers. Finally, rebind is a LLM being developed for reading for fun that helps readers break down the more confusing concepts and engage with the reader. 

Brief Description of a Solution Being Provided

The AI-based tool we are developing will leverage an existing language model, built by another team working on CCEL, to modernize classic Christian texts, generate commentaries, and enhance search capabilities alongside interactive quizzes. By utilizing this pre-developed model, we aim to address key challenges, such as understanding archaic language, engaging with complex theological material, and promoting active learning.

Updating Archaic Language:
The language model will update outdated and difficult-to-understand language in classic Christian texts, making them more accessible to modern readers without altering the original meaning. This directly addresses the issue of comprehension barriers posed by archaic language, allowing users to engage more deeply with the content.

Generating Commentaries:

For more complex or dense theological texts, the model will automatically generate commentaries by analyzing passages and providing insightful explanations and clarifications. This will help readers better understand challenging concepts, reducing the reliance on external sources for comprehension, and enabling deeper engagement with the material.

Enhanced Search Capabilities:

The model will enable users to ask targeted questions or search for specific topics, quotes, or themes within the text by simply providing a prompt. This feature will enhance the ability to navigate and extract meaningful information from large volumes of Christian literature, addressing the issue of limited AI reading tools.

Interactive Checkpoint Quizzes:

To promote active learning and retention, the tool will generate quizzes at the end of each chapter. These quizzes will help reinforce the reader’s understanding of the material, encouraging them to reflect on what they’ve read and engage critically with the text, rather than passively consuming it.
Research or Development Objective
This project is a hybrid of research and development, aiming to evaluate and expand the application of large language models (LLMs) in enhancing the accessibility and understanding of classic Christian texts. Specifically, the research aspect focuses on the following objectives:
Modernizing text language: This aspect investigates how LLMs can transform archaic language into modern, easily understandable English while preserving the text's original meaning.

Creating commentaries: We assess the LLM’s ability to produce insightful and contextually accurate commentaries on complex theological texts, aiding in deeper understanding.

Improving search functionality: This aspect explores how effectively LLMs can enhance search capabilities within large text collections, particularly in locating relevant information in classic Christian literature based on user queries.

Development Aspect: Implement prompt engineering, create interfaces, and create interactive checkpoint quizzes.
Prompt engineering: Creating and testing effective prompts that guide the language model to produce accurate and meaningful responses.
Interface creation: Developing a user-friendly interface that allows readers to easily interact with the tool, search texts, and access generated commentaries.
Interactive checkpoint quizzes: Designing and implementing quizzes at key points in the reading process to encourage active learning and ensure comprehension.
